Introduction to Technologies

Information technology is an important factor in current education because it provides new means and methods of learning. In the care of the early childhood education, the use of technology enhances creative, problem solving and communication skills. Engaging young children to technology resources that are age-appropriate enhances their ability to be conversant with technology-enabled environment (Clements & Sarama, 2003).

Materials:

Some of the technological resources which fit best in the early childhood learning include age appropriate tablets, interactive whiteboards and appropriate educational software. These tools enhance the learning intercourse hence making it embraceable (Clements & Sarama, 2003). Digital Tools: A large number of applications and websites that can be used in educational purposes are focused on helping children diversify activities related to a particular subject as a subject. It should be noted that these tools can fit as learning aids and enhance independent study based on conventional learning resources (Hirsch-Pasek et al. , 2009).

Digital Tools:

As for students, they are better to provide them basic concepts of how to behave in the World Wide Web space responsibly in every aspect. Support helpful direction of the tendencies for teaching children how to become proper virtual citizens and how to protect themselves from dangerous encounters online aids in creating good and safe technology habits (Hirsch-Pasek et al. , 2009).

Learning Experiences:

0-2 Years: Easy technology interfaces such as preschool toys with an application or digital books are examples of how infants can be guided into the technology sphere. This means that the activities which entail touching, swiping, and listening in terms of cognition and motor in the initial years help in the childrenâ€™s development. 2-3 Years: The tools that can assist in enhancing learning for toddlers include the use of more advanced items such as the interactive whiteboards and education games that help babies in identifying basic shapes, colors and numbers. These tools are made to enhance learning through manipulatives that more or less give a technological aspect (Clements & Sarama, 2003). 3-5 Years: As part of social interaction, use of applications and games that require coding and problem solving sums up logical thinking and creativity for children of that tender age of early childhood. All these foster early literacy especially in the digital computing domain as postulated by Bers (2018). 6-8 Years: Sustained technology tasks like construction of simple robots or invention of digital stories So, when children are older allow them to develop more complicated technological abilities. Such activities help in expanding their knowledge on the concept of technology and how it is used (Bers, 2018). Pedagogical Practices: Balanced Integration: Smarter use of technology is essential, but this should be done with correct balance so that technology augments the traditional form of learning. The research also tends to discourage the over reliance on computers as tools for learning but, rather use computers to complement learning (Clements & Sarama, 2003).
