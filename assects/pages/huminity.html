Humanities and Social Sciences

The main idea about Humanities and Social Sciences

Humanities and social science tell children about behaviour, culture, society etc which is important for a child to know about himself/herself and the societies around us. Learning these subjects at an early age makes children become culturally sensitive, and socially sensitive. Using narratives and enacting different scenes as well as going out into the community reading the child’s development in these domains (Dewey 1938).

Materials:

Official and educational web-sites and applications which are connected with history, geography and cultural sciences are also to be considered as the possibilities of further investigation. These tools can complement the conventional educational media and provide the interactive opportunities (Levin & Wadmany, 2008).

Digital Tools:

Books, maps, cultural items, and play clothes are rather necessary to familiarize children with different components of humanities and SS studies. These resources assist children to learn more on different cultures, events and social issues (Nicolopoulou, 2010).

Learning Experiences:

0-2 Years: Songs and gestures regarding other people’s appearance or clothing or events that happen in a family or in a traditional way assist infants in the construction of their perception of the social context. Such experiences help in the development of early social and cultural understanding ( Nicolopoulou, 2010). 2-3 Years: Role-play and storytelling which focuses on the community helpers and family members assist toddlers in identifying the different social positions and tasks. Such practices as dress-up and pretend play help the child to learn all the roles in society (Bredekamp & Copple, 2009). 3-5 Years: Children in early childhood age need to be introduced to various types of books, songs and learning through artwork so that they can embrace world cultures and practices. All these activities make a child embrace Diversity and foster cultural sensitivity because; 6-8 Years: Participation in projects that are connected to certain historical events or local or social topics helps older children to discuss problems and think critically. Collaborations like making maps or even interviews help them improve their learnings in the social studies area (Dewey, 1938). Pedagogical Practices: Inquiry-Based Learning: Motivate children to inquire on topics of fascinating to them and or allow them to choose areas of humanities and social sciences to explore. Some of the advantages of inquiry-based learning include; Critical thinking is encouraged and increased children’s comprehension of different ideas is facilitated (Nicolopoulou, 2010). Cultural Exploration: Include assignments and tasks that teach the students about cultural and tradition differences into lesson plan. In this regard, this approach enables children to embrace diversity and learn how to view issues from other people’s perspectives (Levin & Wadmany, 2008).
