Introduction to Movement and Music in Early Childhood Education

Movement and Music

In this section, the general idea of Movement and Music will be explained.

Motor activity and music are of great importance in young children learning, development of their physical skills, and emotional growth. Rhythmic and musical activities assist children in establishing good coordination, rhythm as well gain joy from the activity (Whitehead, 2010). These activities also help creative freedom and social skills to be learnt and practised in the process.

Materials:

Remaining of voice, including musical instruments movement props such as scarves or hoops, and music apps are required in enhancing movement and music experience (Eisner, 2002). These materials enable children work with different sounds and movements.

Pedagogical Practices:

Integration into Daily Routines: Incorporate educational movement to the different activities including using music while learning. Musical activities can be integrated into the daily life scenarios, which in turn benefits for children’s learning and their development, as stated by Hallam (2010). Movement and Learning: Help the child to master various learning topics through movement activities to improve his knowledge. According to Whitehead (2010), teaching techniques such as dancing and music help to bring more fun to the learning process.

Learning Experiences:

0-2 Years: Playing with musical instruments including drums or maracas is essential in helping develop the infant’s auditory and motor coordination. We offer baby tapping and shaking toys, which help give sensory input and support the early brain develo pment of rhythm (Gordon, 2003). 2-3 Years: Using accessories such as scarves and hoops will make the toddlers have more interaction and gain more coordination. These are exercises that enable children to try various action and body postures and singing and dancing (Barrett, 2012). 3-5 Years: Listening to a variety of music and engaging in group music projects and singing benefit early childhood children musical development and learning. Listening to and composing music helps children to learn variety of musical genres and encourage the process of imagination (Kraus, 2016). 6-8 Years: To do their own songs and dances, older children get to have more ways in which they can learn better and have more fun while at the same time coming up with ways to have their bodies and brains develop much more. These activities help children to try themselves in creation of the musical and choreographic pieces improving artistic skills (Hallam, 2010).
