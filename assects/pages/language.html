Language and Literacy

Social drama for health promotion in schools

Oral language and writing readiness are developmental domains in the early years of growth and development these early education domains make children to have better communication skills. Implementation of language and literacy activities in early age enables the children to build up proper reading and writing skills, which are vital foundation upon which they shall base their academic achievements in future (Clay 2005).

Materials:

These items include books, items to play literacy games and writing instruments to support language as well as literacy development. Hethereby enables children to use language in a number and ways as they work towards being provided with number of literacy resources (Morrow, 2005).

Digital Tools:

-books and apps related to literacy give more opportunities for further practicing in language and literacy skills. Such tools can be used in coordination with conventional reading and writing lessons, and offer learning experiences that have not previously been available (Neuman & Celano, 2001).

Learning Experiences:

0-2 Years: Sensory story time is a useful tool in encouraging early reading along with the use of sensory books to help the children in their play. Picture books especially those with textures, flaps, and those that one can touch and turn help infants and develop their language development (Beck & McKeown, 2001). 2-3 Years: The use of picture books in storytelling helps improveToddler’s vocabulary as well as comprehension level. It is found that beginning with interactive dialogue shared during involvement in participating in interactive read-alouds the students are provided with best chance to develop their love for reading as well as their language skills as observed by Whitehurst et al. (1988). 3-5 Years: It means that through writing and creating stories, the preschoolers may increase the level of literacy and their creativity. A child’s ability to dictate or to write his or her own stories promotes creation of dual frameworks as well as enhances the child’s attention toward the narrative structure (Hargrave and Sénéchal, 2000). 6-8 Years: More complex work related to deciphering texts is a test for the young reader with the increase in the child’s age. Reading activities including the summarizing and making predictions and inferences helps the children in improving on their critical thinking as well as reading skills (Chall, 1983). Pedagogical Practices: Literacy-Rich Environment: Ensure that the children are surrounded by literacy by providing them with the stimuli to read and write. Morrow (2005) observed that exposure to multiple Literacy resources as well as integrating literacy on the environment across event and routines promote language development among children. Interactive Methods: Ensure that there is incorporation of bodily-kinesthetic and other relevant aspects to help in skills acquisition. As to special activities, shared reading, telling and writing workshops emphasize the furthering of language abilities and generating of interest in reading (Neuman & Celano, 2001).
